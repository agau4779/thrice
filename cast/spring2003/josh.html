Why did you get involved with Theatre Rice?
My friend Joyce, who's a former ricer now, recommended that I come check out a meeting. So I showed up but I had many doubts...and I left early. Then suddenly, I made up my mind to join 15 minutes later and ran back to sign up for an audition. I haven't regreted it since!

What did you expect to gain from TR?
I've already gained so many aspects of theatre rice---life long friendships, a strong confidence in myself, leadership skills, trust, teamwork, lack of sleep, being a better actor, theater techniques and making people think, cry, and laugh at issues important to all of us.

Where do you see yourself in five years?
I see myself building up my career in the entertainment industry as an aspiring actor or model. I've always had this dream of helping people, and through these outlets, I can speak creatively for the unheard through film, tv, and whatever medium I can contribute toward the good of the world. I know that's naive, but that's the type of person I'm becoming.

What makes you smile?
I smile when I experience spontaneous moments with people I care about.( I love to watch shooting stars and share stories! ) I get thrilled when I come across someone I haven't seen in a long time or even going on rare trips back to where I grew up--Guam!! I do enjoy helping people any way I can...and making them smile if they are down. Since I'm a huge fun, watching Buffy and Smallville makes me smile a lot too!

Do you have any special talents?
I can speak in different voices and produce many animated facial expressions. I am also a natural at drawing freehand, writing poetry, designing graphic art and my personal webpage. I know some of my native language, which is called Chamorro. I have been told my positive attitude and niceness are special talents. I guess I heal people with my words.
