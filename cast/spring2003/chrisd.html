Why did you get involved with Theatre Rice?
I have always wanted to get involved in theatre since I was in high school. High school drama was really cliquish so I always felt really alienated and excluded from doing drama in high school. I saw Theatre Rice’s crazy flyering on Sproul my first semester at Berkeley and I decided to join the group without even having seen a show.

What did you expect to gain from TR?
I have already gained so much from Theatre Rice in terms of skills that I can use in everyday life (i.e. performing onstage has greatly improved my public speaking abilities). I hope to further develop these skills and to gain new ones as well. Oh yeah, I’m also looking for a good time every Tuesday and Thursday evening.

Where do you see yourself in five years?
Probably still at CAL (“Hi, I’m Chris, and I’m an eighth year, undeclared.”) It’d be nice if one could make a living as a professional, full-time student. But alas, I’ll probably be finally going out into the “real world.” Then again, I’ve always wanted to go to culinary school, so maybe I’ll be over in New York or something (in the kitchen and on off-off-off-off Broadway productions).

Who are your role models?
Chairman Takeshi Kaga of Kitchen Stadium. It has always been my dream to own a Kitchen Stadium (with my own army of Iron Chefs), wear flamboyant, sequined suits, and to eat fried conger eel heads smothered in vanilla ice cream. Aaaaah, now that would be the good life.

What are your favorite movies?
Currently, my favorite film is Darren Aronofsky’s Requiem for a Dream. I also used to be a really hardcore Star Wars fan and the ORIGINAL trilogy of films still has a place in my heart.
