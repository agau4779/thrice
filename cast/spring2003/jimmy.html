Why did you get involved with Theatre Rice?
To share our stories (as Asian Americans) with other Asian Americans in hopes of inspiring each other and creating a stronger community of support and solidarity amongst Asian Americans of different Ethnicities (Yes, contrary to popular conception Asian America is very diverse and the Asian American umbrella label is misleading). But as Asian Americans, we simply get no love from mainstream media (Better Luck Tomorrow is only a start but that too is limited release only available to those who were seeking it in the first place); our stories, our histories are so silent, only to be found in the few privileged college campuses that have Asian American history books with professors and even then, the resources are tokenistic. We all have something to say, to share, so DOO IT because we’re never given the chance so here’s your chance…

Where do you see yourself in five years?
Even higher (graduate school) education by day, artist (theater/writing/poet/comedian) by night. Either in Film/Creative Writing/Ethnic Studies/Education graduate school, teaching English abroad or at community college, poetry slam circuit, making a movie or on a Hawaiian beach running my own night cafe and surfing during the day.

What makes you smile?
Talking about the future of Theatre Rice, creating/seeing great ideas flourish into something even more amazing, seeing people drunk and happy, dad/mom/parent playing with their children, being on the verge of overcoming an obstacle on a video game, a great LOMO, everyone around me dying of laughter, knowing/hearing that I have inspired someone, life and its funny ways.
